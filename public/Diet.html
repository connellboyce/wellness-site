Diet


A balanced diet is the most esstenial in our daily life. It gives your body the nutrients it needs to function and work effectively. Without it, our body is prone to disease, infection, fatigue, and poor performance. A way to get the proper nutrition if from your diet. While there are many different diets, it's important to find the right diet that works best for you! There are many different diets and different ways on how it can be achieved.

Below list are different diets with the recommended foods to consume.
(Disclaimer: Not all diets/foods will be effective, everyone’s body is different and will react differently, if you have a food allergy make sure to check with your doctor before following the list below.)

The main way to achieve a balanced diet is by consuming foods that are low in unecessary fats and sugars and high in vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients. There are differnt food groups that are essential part of a balanced diet.

  1. Fruits:
    Fruits are high in sugar and the sugar is natural. Fruits is a better choice for your health than other foods with added sugar. It's important to watch your sugar intake, especially those who have diabetes, who need to opt for low-sugar fruits instead.

  2. Vegtables:
    Vegatables is the primary source of essential vitamins and minerals. For example, dark and leafy greens generally contain the most nutrition and it can be eaten with any meal. It's important to eat a variety of vegatbles which will help you obtain a lot of nutrients that vegetables can provide.

  3. Grains:
    Grains are important source os many nutrients, including fiber, vitamin B, and minerals. Dietary fiber from whole grains is part of the most healthy diet. It can help reduce blood cholesterol levels and may lower the risk of heart disease. Fiber is the important for healthy bowel function and helps reduce constipation.

  4. Protiens:
    Meats and beans are the primary source of protein. This is a nutrient that is essential for proper muscle and brain development. For example, lean, low-fat meats such as chicken, fish, and certain cuts of pork and beef are the best options. Removing skin and trimming any excess fat is an easy way to reduce the amoutn of fat and cholesterol intake.

  5. Dairy:
    Dairy products provides calcium, vitamin D, and other essential nutrients. But, the are the major source of fat and it important to watch how much you intake. So, try to have small portions of any dairy products. There are many different dairy products such as plant-based, almond, and soy.

The low-carb, whole-food diet is perfect for people who need to lose weight, optimize health, and lower their risk of disease. It’s flexible, allowing you to fine-tune your carb intake depending on your goals. This diet is high in vegetables, meat, fish, eggs, fruits, nuts, and fats but low in starches, sugars, and processed foods.

Food to Avoid:

  1. Sugar:
    Soft drinks, fruit juices, agave, candy, ice cream and many other products that contain added sugar.

  2. Refined grains:
    Wheat, rice, barley and rye, as well as bread, cereal and pasta.

  3. Diet and low-fat products:
    Many dairy products, cereals or crackers are fat-reduced, but contain added sugar.

  4. Highly processed foods:
    If it looks like it was made in a factory, don't eat it.

  5. Starchy vegetables:
    It’s best to limit starchy vegetables in your diet if you’re following a very low-carb diet.

The gluten-free diet is essential for people who are intolerant to gluten, a protein that is found in wheat, rye, and barley. For optimal health, you should focus on whole foods that are naturally gluten-free. Gluten-free junk food is still junk food.

The vegan diet has become increasingly popular in the past decade. It’s linked to a number of health benefits, including weight loss, improved heart health, and better blood sugar control. The diet is based exclusively on plant foods and eliminates all animal products.
