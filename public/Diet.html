Diet


A balanced diet is the most esstenial in our daily life. It gives your body the nutrients it needs to function and work effectively. Without it, our body is prone to disease, infection, fatigue, and poor performance. A way to get the proper nutrition if from your diet. While there are many different diets, it's important to find the right diet that works best for you! There are many different diets and different ways on how it can be achieved.

Below list are different diets with the recommended foods to consume.
(Disclaimer: Not all diets/foods will be effective, everyone’s body is different and will react differently, if you have a food allergy make sure to check with your doctor before following the list below.)


The main way to achieve a balanced diet is by consuming foods that are low in unecessary fats and sugars and high in vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients. There are different food groups that are essential part of a balanced diet.

Essential Food Groups:
  1. Fruits: Fruits are high in sugar and the sugar is natural. Fruits is a better choice for your health than other foods with added sugar. It's important to watch your sugar intake, especially those who have diabetes, who need to opt for low-sugar fruits instead.

  2. Vegtables: Vegatables is the primary source of essential vitamins and minerals. For example, dark and leafy greens generally contain the most nutrition and it can be eaten with any meal. It's important to eat a variety of vegatbles which will help you obtain a lot of nutrients that vegetables can provide.

  3. Grains: Grains are important source os many nutrients, including fiber, vitamin B, and minerals. Dietary fiber from whole grains is part of the most healthy diet. It can help reduce blood cholesterol levels and may lower the risk of heart disease. Fiber is the important for healthy bowel function and helps reduce constipation.

  4. Protiens: Meats and beans are the primary source of protein. This is a nutrient that is essential for proper muscle and brain development. For example, lean, low-fat meats such as chicken, fish, and certain cuts of pork and beef are the best options. Removing skin and trimming any excess fat is an easy way to reduce the amoutn of fat and cholesterol intake.

  5. Dairy: Dairy products provides calcium, vitamin D, and other essential nutrients. But, the are the major source of fat and it important to watch how much you intake. So, try to have small portions of any dairy products. There are many different dairy products such as plant-based, almond, and soy.


The low-carb, whole-food diet is perfect for people who need to lose weight, optimize health, and lower their risk of disease. It’s flexible, allowing you to fine-tune your carb intake depending on your goals. This diet is high in vegetables, meat, fish, eggs, fruits, nuts, and fats but low in starches, sugars, and processed foods.

Foods to Eat:
  1. Meat: Beef, lamb, pork, chicken and others; grass-fed is best.


  2. Fish: Salmon, trout, haddock and many others; wild-caught fish is best.


  3. Eggs: Omega-3-enriched or pastured eggs are best.


  4. Vegtables: Spinach, broccoli, cauliflower, carrots and many others.


  5. Fruits: Apples, oranges, pears, blueberries, strawberries.


  6. Tubers: Potatoes, sweet potatoes, yams, turnips, etc.


  7. Nuts and Seeds: Almonds, walnuts, sunflower seeds, etc.


  8. High-Fat Dairy: Cheese, butter, heavy cream, yogurt.


  9. Fats and Oils: Coconut oil, butter, lard, olive oil and fish oil.


Foods to Avoid:

  1. Sugar: Soft drinks, fruit juices, agave, candy, ice cream and many other products that contain added sugar.


  2. Refined Grains: Wheat, rice, barley and rye, as well as bread, cereal and pasta.


  3. Diet and Low-Fast Products: Many dairy products, cereals or crackers are fat-reduced, but contain added sugar.


  4. Highly Processed Foods: Everything labeled "diet" or "low-fat" or that has many additives. Includes artificial meal replacements.


  5. Trans Fats: Hydrogenated or partially hydrogenated oils.


  6. Starchy Vegatables: It’s best to limit starchy vegetables in your diet if you’re following a very low-carb diet.



The paleo diet is a natural way of eating that excludes all intake of sugar. The other sugar that paleo diet intakes are form fruits. However, sugar is not the only thing that is abandon, there is also an elimination of processed foods, grains and dairy.

Foods to Eat:
  1. Meat: Beef, lamb, chicken, turkey, pork and others.


  2. Fish and Seafood: Salmon, trout, haddock, shrimp, shellfish, etc. Choose wild-caught if you can.


  3. Eggs: Choose free-range, pastured or omega-3 enriched eggs.


  4. Vegtables: Broccoli, kale, peppers, onions, carrots, tomatoes, etc.


  5. Fruits: Apples, bananas, oranges, pears, avocados, strawberries, blueberries and more.


  6. Tubers: Potatoes, sweet potatoes, yams, turnips, etc.


  7. Nuts and Seeds: Almonds, macadamia nuts, walnuts, hazelnuts, sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds and more.


  8. Healthy Fats and Oils: Extra virgin olive oil, coconut oil, avocado oil and others.


  9. Salt and Spices: Sea salt, garlic, turmeric, rosemary, etc.


Foods to Avoid:

  1. Sugar and High-Fructose Corn Syrup: Soft drinks, fruit juices, table sugar, candy, pastries, ice cream and many others.


  2. Grains: Includes breads and pastas, wheat, spelt, rye, barley, etc.


  3. Most Dairy Products: Especially low-fat.


  4. Legumes: Beans, lentils and many more.


  5. Artifical Sweetners: Aspartame, sucralose, cyclamates, saccharin, acesulfame potassium. Stick to natural sweeteners instead.


  6. Vegatable Oils: Soybean oil, sunflower oil, cottonseed oil, corn oil, grapeseed oil, safflower oil and others.


  7. Highly Processed Foods: Everything labeled "diet" or "low-fat" or that has many additives. Includes artificial meal replacements.


  8. Trans Fat: Found in margarine and various processed foods. Usually referred to as "hydrogenated" or "partially hydrogenated" oils.




The vegan diet has become increasingly popular in the past decade. It’s linked to a number of health benefits, including weight loss, improved heart health, and better blood sugar control. The diet is based exclusively on plant foods and eliminates all animal products.


Foods to Eat:
  1. Tofu, Tempeh and Seitan: These provide a versatile protein-rich alternative to meat, fish, poultry and eggs in many recipes.


  2. Legumes: Beans, lentils and peas are excellent sources of many nutrients and beneficial plant compounds.


  3. Nuts and Nut Butters: Unblanched and unroasted varieties, which are good sources of iron, fiber, magnesium, zinc, selenium and vitamin E.


  4. Calcium-Fortified Plant Milks and Yogurts: Opt for varieties fortified with vitamins B12 and D.


  5. Fruits and Vegtables: Leafy greens such as bok choy, spinach, kale, watercress and mustard greens are particularly high in iron and calcium.


  6. Algae: Spirulina and chlorella are good sources of complete protein. Other varieties are great sources of iodine.


  7. Seeds: Hemp, chia and flaxseeds, which contain a good amount of protein and beneficial omega-3 fatty acids


  8. Nutritional Yeast: increase the protein content of vegan dishes and add an interesting cheesy flavor. Pick vitamin B12-fortified varieties whenever possible.


  9. Whole Grains, Cereals and Pseudocereals: A great source of complex carbs, fiber, iron, B-vitamins and several minerals. Spelt, teff, amaranth and quinoa are especially high-protein options.


  10. Sprouted and Fermented Plant Foods: Ezekiel bread, tempeh, miso, natto, sauerkraut, pickles, kimchi and kombucha often contain probiotics and vitamin K2.


Foods to Avoid:

  1. Meat and Poultry: Beef, lamb, pork, veal, horse, organ meat, wild meat, chicken, turkey, goose, duck, quail, etc.


  2. Fish and Seafood: All types of fish, anchovies, shrimp, squid, scallops, calamari, mussels, crab, lobster, etc.


  3. Dairy Products: Milk, yogurt, cheese, butter, cream, ice cream, etc.


  4. Eggs: From chickens, quails, ostriches, fish, etc.


  5. Bee Products: Honey, bee pollen, royal jelly, etc.


  6. Animal-based ingredients: Whey, casein, lactose, egg white albumen, gelatin, cochineal or carmine, isinglass, shellac, L-cysteine, animal-derived vitamin D3 and fish-derived omega-3 fatty acids.
