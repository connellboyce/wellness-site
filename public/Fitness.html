Fitness


Physical activity or daily to weekly excercise is very important and can be beneficial to your health. It can help reduce risk of developing diseases such as diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular disease. By excercising, you can have immediate and long-term health benefits, plus can improve your quality of life. Sometimes we will tell ourselves that we don't have the time, but even just 30 minutes a days can make a huge difference to your lifestyle!

Below list are different workouts you can follow.
(Disclaimer: Not all workouts below will fit you and may not be effective, everyone’s body is different and will react differently, make sure to do more research on the type of workout you are looking for before following the steps below.)


Bulking, in fitness terms, is when you eat more calories than your body needs to maintain its mass, in an effort to supply enough calories to build new muscle mass. Below will give you a beginner guide to a bulk workout. The weight will gradually increase every 2-3 weeks between 1-5kg.

First 2 Weeks:

Day 1: Back
  1. 5 x 8 Barbell Shrug

  2. 5 x 8 Dumbbell Raise

  3. 3 x 8 Deadlift

  4. 5 x 8 Bent Over-Rows

  5. 3 x 6 Pull-Ups

Day 2: Chest
  1. 5 x 6 Bench Press

  2. 5 x 8 Pec Flys

  3. 5 x 6 Incline Bench

  4. 5 x 8 Decline Dumbbell Bench Press

Day 3: Legs
  1. 3 x 8 Barbell Squat

  2. 5 x 8 Leg Extension

  3. 5 x 8 Dumbbell Lunges

  4. 3 x 8 Front Squats

Day 4: Arms and Shoulders
  1. 3 x 8 Military Press

  2. 5 x 8 EZ Bar Bicep Curls

  3. 5 x 8 Overhead Tricep Extensions

  4. 3 x 8 Chin-Ups



Cutting is where a person reduces their calorie intake and increases the amount of cardiovascular exercise in order to reduce their body fat and therefore increasing muscle definition. If you want to get big, and pack on muscle, then you should focus on lifting heavy weights for low repetitions. Below will give you a beginner guide to a bulk workout. The weight will gradually increase every couple of weeks.

First 2 Weeks:

Day 1: Legs and Calves
  1. 4 x 8 Squats

  2. 4 x 8 Romanian Deadlifts

  3. 4 x 8 Barbell Hack Squat

  4. 4 x 8 Split Squats

  5. 4 x 8 Seated Calf Raise

  6. 5 x 15 Seated Calf Raise

Day 2: Back and Biceps
  1. 4 x 8 Pull Ups

  2. 4 x 8 Pendlay Rows

  3. 4 x 8 Single Arm Rows

  4. 3 x 8 Barbell Curl

  5. 3 x 8 Supinating Dumbbell Curl

Day 3: Chest and Triceps
  1. 4 x 8 Barbell Flat Bench Press

  2. 4 x 8 Incline Dumbbell Close Press

  3. 4 x 8 Chest Dips

  4. 4 x 8 Seated Dumbbell Single Arm French Press

  5. 3 x 8 Reverse Grip Skull Crushers

Day 4: Trapezius and Shoulders
  1. 4 x 8 Overhead Press

  2. 4 x 8 Single Arm Arnold Press

  3. 4 x 8 Barbell Face Pulls

  4. 3 x 8 Lateral Raises

  5. 3 x 8 Barbell Shrugs



Supplements such as vitamins, minerals, essential fatty acids, phytochemicals, and more enrich the body's internal environment to fortify cellular protection, repair, and regeneration and support the Renewal process. However, make sure to not take supplements as a way to replace a diet, but instead to aid it.

Types of Supplements:

  1. Protien Shakes: Protien shakes is a great way to increase protein consumptions by mixing powder with milk in a blender. There are two type sof protein such as whey and casein. A great way to benefit in a bulking diet is by taking a casin protein shake before bed or a whey protein shake after a workout.



  2. Multivitamins: Sometimes it is impossible to obtain all the vitamins and minerals from the foods we eat that our body needs daily. However, multivitamins help fill this issue and by taking it daily, it can help keep our body running at high efficiency.


  3. Creatine: Creatine is a naturally used in your body to increase ATP production, which is energy for your muscles. When bulking up, the body requires more creatine than what you consume from meals. So, the best way to obtain more creatine is by taking creatine supplements to fuel your muscles when lifting.


  4. Ready to Drink Shakes: Similar to protien shakes, this is served as a meal addition or replacement. This is great for on the go when your don't have time to prepare fix meals every couple of hours.


  5. Nitric Oxide: Nitric Oxide helps increase nutrient delivery to the muscles and beneficial for during or post workout. Taking nitric oxide supplements will allow you to be able to lift more weights for a longer period of time and help repair your muscles after workout.
